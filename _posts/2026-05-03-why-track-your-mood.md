It’s easy to let days blur together, especially when you are stressed. You might think, *"I've been feeling awful all week,"* but is that actually true? 

Tracking your mood does two massive things for your mental health:

**1. It breaks the "All-or-Nothing" illusion**
When we have a bad day, our brains trick us into thinking *every* day is bad. Looking back at a visual graph of your mood proves that you actually had a pretty good Tuesday, even if Thursday was rough. It gives you perspective. In addition to making you have less of a pessimistic mind

**2. It helps you find your triggers**
By logging *why* you feel a certain way (like "Sleep" or "School/Work"), you start to see patterns. You might realize your mood consistently crashes on days you get less than 6 hours of sleep, or that socializing actually boosts your mood more than you thought.

You can't fix what you don't understand. Taking 30 seconds a day to check in is the first step to figuring out what your mind actually needs.

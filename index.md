Mental health is one of those things everyone knows matters, but most people ignore until it starts affecting everything else around them. It doesn’t usually hit all at once either, it builds up slowly. Stress, overthinking, random bad days, all of it stacks.

This site is here to help with that.

It gives you a simple space to check in with yourself. Nothing complicated. Just tracking how you feel, what affected your day, and getting thoughts out of your head instead of letting them sit there. Bottling up is one of the biggest causes of depression, people rarely trust each other now and they can't be blamed for that either. 

Over time, you start seeing patterns. What ruins your mood. What actually helps. What days are consistently worse. Once you see that clearly, it’s easier to deal with instead of just reacting to everything.

### Why This Matters

Mental health isn’t optional. It’s part of your overall health, whether people take it seriously or not. Ignoring it doesn’t make it go away, it just makes things harder later.

This project also connects to the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 3 (Good Health and Well-being), which focuses on improving overall health and promoting well-being for people everywhere. Mental health is a big part of that, even if it doesn’t always get the same attention as physical health.

You don’t need to be going through something serious to use this. Even on normal days, taking a minute to check in with yourself can help you stay more in control.

### How to Use This Site

**Daily Check-in:**  
Rate your mood from 1 to 10 and write a short note about your day. It will be there for you to remember that day at any time. Good or bad.

**History:**  
Go back and see your past entries. This helps you notice patterns in your mood over time.

**The Void:**  
Write whatever you want and let it disappear. Nothing is saved, so you can just let things out and move on.

**Posts:**  
Coming soon. This will include useful content and resources.

Start with the Daily Check-in and don’t overthink it. The simpler you keep it, the more useful it becomes.
